# Audience Builder

## Purpose
Create detailed, actionable Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) documents that inform every other EMOVEL skill — ensuring all copy, offers, funnels, visuals, and pricing are calibrated to the exact person being served.

## Inputs
- Business type and industry
- Product or service category
- Any existing customer data: testimonials, demographics, purchase history
- Pain points or problems the product solves
- Competitor audience descriptions (optional)
- Desired market position: mass market, niche, micro-niche, ultra-premium

## Outputs
- ICP name and avatar summary (1 paragraph)
- Demographic profile (age, gender, income, location, education, job title)
- Psychographic profile (values, beliefs, aspirations, fears, identity)
- Daily life snapshot (what a day looks like, pain moments, hope moments)
- Top 3 problems (stated in the audience's own language)
- Top 3 desires (outcome language, not feature language)
- Biggest objections to buying
- Trusted information sources (where they consume content)
- Buying behavior (impulse vs. research, price sensitivity, brand loyalty)
- Emotional triggers (what makes them act)
- Language patterns (exact phrases they use to describe their problem)
- Anti-avatar (who this offer is NOT for)

## Rules
- ICP must be specific enough to reject 80% of the market and perfectly serve 20%
- Demographics without psychographics are useless — both are required
- Language patterns must be sourced from real customer language (reviews, forums, comments), not assumed
- Anti-avatar section is mandatory — knowing who to repel sharpens positioning
- Pain and desire descriptions must use the audience's vocabulary, not the brand's vocabulary
- Emotional triggers must name the specific feeling, not a category (e.g., "fear of being judged by peers" not just "fear")

## Workflow
1. Receive business type, product, and any existing customer data
2. Define demographic profile
3. Build psychographic profile (values, beliefs, identity)
4. Write daily life snapshot (narrative paragraph)
5. Identify top 3 problems in audience's own language
6. Identify top 3 desires as outcome statements
7. List top objections to purchase
8. Map information consumption sources
9. Define buying behavior and price sensitivity
10. List emotional trigger words
11. Write anti-avatar profile
12. Deliver complete ICP document

## Examples
- ICP for a $297 online course on freelance copywriting (target: burned-out 9-to-5 employee, 28–42)
- ICP for a premium Shopify skincare brand (target: wellness-oriented women, 30–50, household income $80K+)
- ICP for a B2B SaaS project management tool (target: ops managers at 10–50 person agencies)
- ICP for a high-ticket coaching program ($5K+) targeting struggling entrepreneurs
