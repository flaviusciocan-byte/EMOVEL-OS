# Product Structure

## Product Type

Premium digital product and productized service hybrid.

EMOVEL Launch Stack v1 can be sold as a self-serve system, a done-with-you sprint, or an agency-facing white-label workflow.

## Audience Snapshot

### ICP Name

**The Underlaunched Operator**

### Avatar Summary

The Underlaunched Operator is a founder, creator, consultant, or small agency owner with a product idea that is useful but not yet packaged. They can build, teach, advise, or deliver, but their launch stalls because the offer, price, page, and content plan are unclear.

### Demographics

| Attribute | Profile |
|---|---|
| Age | 27 to 45 |
| Income | $50K to $250K annual personal or business income |
| Location | US, UK, EU, Canada, Australia, remote-first markets |
| Role | Founder, solo operator, consultant, creator, agency owner |
| Education | Self-taught or college educated, comfortable with digital tools |
| Business stage | Idea, pre-launch, early traction, or relaunch |

### Psychographics

- Values speed, clarity, ownership, and leverage.
- Wants to look premium without hiring a full launch team.
- Feels embarrassed when the product is strong but the market message is weak.
- Believes AI should create usable business assets, not vague suggestions.
- Wants a launch package that feels sharp enough to show publicly.

### Daily Life Snapshot

They spend the day switching between product work, client work, content ideas, and tool experiments. They have notes in multiple places and a rough sense that the product could sell, but each attempt to write the page creates more uncertainty. The painful moment comes when they realize they can describe the product to themselves but not to a buyer.

### Top Problems In Their Language

1. "I know what it does, but I cannot explain it in a way that sells."
2. "I have no idea what to charge without sounding cheap or delusional."
3. "I keep rebuilding the idea instead of launching it."

### Top Desires

1. A product offer they can confidently send to a real buyer.
2. A clean page structure that makes the product feel premium.
3. A launch plan that tells them exactly what to publish and when.

### Objections

- "Will this be too generic?"
- "Can AI understand my product well enough?"
- "I already have ChatGPT. Why pay for this?"
- "What if my idea is not ready?"
- "Will this actually help me sell?"

### Trusted Sources

- Founder newsletters
- YouTube build-in-public channels
- Indie hacker communities
- LinkedIn operators
- X/Twitter product builders
- Gumroad, Lemon Squeezy, Framer, Webflow, and AI tool ecosystems

### Buying Behavior

They will research before buying, but they make fast decisions when the offer feels specific, practical, and premium. They are moderately price sensitive under $500, and highly sensitive to anything that smells like generic AI prompts.

### Emotional Triggers

- Relief from finally having a clear launch plan
- Pride in seeing the idea framed professionally
- Fear of wasting another month in planning mode
- Desire to look serious in front of peers and buyers
- Urgency around turning unused ideas into revenue

### Language Patterns

- "I need to get this out of my head."
- "I need the offer to feel premium."
- "I do not want another prompt pack."
- "I want something I can actually use."
- "I am stuck between idea and launch."

### Anti-Avatar

This is not for people who want passive income without doing real product work, buyers looking for a magic revenue guarantee, teams with no product idea at all, or enterprise companies that need months of brand research and stakeholder approvals.

## Product Modules

### Module 1: Product Intake

Captures the raw idea, audience, transformation, constraints, proof, assets, and launch goals.

Output: Completed launch brief.

### Module 2: Audience Definition

Uses `emovel.audience_builder` to define the buyer, objections, language patterns, triggers, and anti-avatar.

Output: Audience Snapshot.

### Module 3: Offer Architecture

Uses `emovel.offer_architect` to turn the idea into a transformation-led offer with bonuses, guarantee, value stack, urgency, and price anchor.

Output: Offer Stack.

### Module 4: Pricing Strategy

Uses `emovel.pricing_engine` to define core price, tiers, payment plan, upgrade path, and future price increase milestones.

Output: Pricing Map.

### Module 5: Landing Page System

Uses `emovel.page_builder` and `emovel.copy_framework` to create a sales page structure with conversion copy and objection handling.

Output: Landing Page Copy.

### Module 6: Visual Direction

Uses `emovel.visual_brief` to create a design brief for the page, social assets, PDF assets, and launch visuals.

Output: Visual Brief.

### Module 7: Funnel And Launch Plan

Uses `emovel.funnel_builder` to map the entry point, email sequence, sales page, checkout, upsell, content plan, and KPIs.

Output: Launch Plan.

## Production Flow

1. Complete product intake.
2. Build audience profile.
3. Build offer and value stack.
4. Set pricing and tiers.
5. Draft landing page copy.
6. Define visual system.
7. Map funnel and launch sequence.
8. Package outputs into a launch-ready folder.

## Minimum Viable Product

The smallest sellable version includes:

- Intake form
- Audience Snapshot
- Offer Stack
- Pricing Map
- Landing Page Copy
- Visual Brief
- 14-Day Launch Plan

Out of scope for v1:

- Building the live landing page
- Payment processor setup
- Ad account setup
- Full brand identity design
- Revenue guarantee
- Custom market research beyond buyer-supplied context

## Recommended Delivery Format

- One Google Doc or PDF for client-facing delivery
- One internal markdown folder for EMOVEL production
- Optional Framer/Webflow wireframe in future versions
- Optional Notion dashboard for agency clients
