# Production UX Audit

## Direction

The page uses the EMOVEL "premium launch command system" brief: high contrast, restrained motion, table-led deliverables, process strip, and a concrete launch-stack artifact in the first viewport. The page's one job is to move an underlaunched founder from uncertainty to purchase intent for the Core $497 offer.

## Section Hierarchy

1. Hero: transformation, CTA, and launch-stack artifact.
2. Problem: clarify the stalled-launch pain.
3. System: show the seven-skill production sequence.
4. Deliverables: table of concrete assets.
5. Benefits: outcome-led reasons to buy.
6. Proof: honest system proof with a 48-hour specificity anchor.
7. Pricing: Starter/Core/Premium tier decision.
8. Guarantee: risk reversal near pricing.
9. FAQ: objection handling.
10. Closing CTA: repeat the transformation.

## Accessibility Notes

- Primary CTA is above the fold and has a 48px minimum height.
- Focus states are visible.
- Motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- The page uses semantic sections, headings, tables-as-layout avoided in code, and native `details` for FAQ.
- Color usage follows the visual brief palette.

## Anti-Patterns Avoided

- No fake testimonial avatars.
- No purple gradient hero.
- No decorative particle/AI background.
- No generic six-icon feature grid.
- No fake scarcity countdown.
