# Visual Brief

## Brand Personality

EMOVEL Launch Stack v1 should feel like a premium operator's workspace: sharp, structured, calm, and commercially serious. The brand should communicate leverage and clarity rather than hype. It should feel more like a high-end launch command center than a generic AI template bundle.

The visual tone should make buyers feel that their idea is being turned into a real market asset. The system should look trustworthy enough for consultants and agencies, but fast and modern enough for solo founders and creators.

## Visual Style Descriptor

**Premium launch command system**

Clean, structured, high-contrast, editorial, and productized. Avoid playful AI novelty styling.

## Color Palette

| Role | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Primary Ink | `#101114` | Headlines, main text, nav |
| Deep Graphite | `#20242B` | Dark sections, footer, premium blocks |
| Signal Blue | `#2F6BFF` | Primary CTA, active states, links |
| Electric Mint | `#40D9A3` | Success accents, checkmarks, metric highlights |
| Soft Cloud | `#F5F7FA` | Page background, section bands |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Cards, content surfaces |
| Line Gray | `#D9DEE7` | Borders, dividers, UI lines |

Contrast rule: Use `#101114` on `#FFFFFF` or `#F5F7FA` for body text. Use white text on `#20242B` for dark bands. Avoid blue body text on dark backgrounds unless contrast is checked.

## Typography

### Heading Font

Inter Tight or Satoshi

Use for:

- Hero headline
- Section headlines
- Pricing numbers
- Short high-impact statements

### Body Font

Inter

Use for:

- Body copy
- FAQ
- Tables
- Product explanations

### Accent Font

IBM Plex Mono

Use sparingly for:

- Skill names
- System labels
- Version tags
- Process steps

## Layout Principles

- Mobile-first, single primary CTA above the fold.
- Use strong section bands instead of nested cards.
- Keep content blocks clean, scannable, and commercially direct.
- Use tables for deliverables and pricing logic.
- Use process strips for the seven-skill workflow.
- Keep radius at 8px or less.
- Use generous whitespace around the hero and pricing sections.
- Avoid decorative visual noise.

## Image Direction

Human presence: **operator-forward, not stock-smiling.**

Use imagery that suggests:

- Founder at a focused workspace
- Product strategy boards
- Launch dashboards
- Clean laptop scenes with notes, page wireframes, and content calendars
- Close crops of hands, notebooks, screens, and launch planning materials

Lighting:

- Crisp natural light or controlled studio light
- Clear details, not moody blur
- Neutral backgrounds with one strong accent color

Composition:

- Organized desk or dashboard views
- Layered documents and page wireframes
- Sharp focus on planning artifacts

Avoid:

- Robot faces
- Neon cyberpunk AI visuals
- Abstract purple gradients
- Overly casual creator lifestyle photos
- Corporate handshake imagery

## Iconography

Use simple line icons with a consistent 1.5px or 2px stroke.

Recommended icon themes:

- Target for audience
- Stack/layers for offer
- Tag or coin for pricing
- Page layout for landing page
- Pen line for copy
- Palette for visual brief
- Funnel for launch system

## Moodboard Directions

1. Clean founder workspace with launch notes and page wireframe.
2. Dark graphite dashboard with bright blue CTA and mint success states.
3. Editorial SaaS landing page with precise typography and clear pricing block.
4. High-end strategy document with tables, labels, and compact callouts.
5. Minimal product launch calendar with content milestones.
6. Figma or Framer-style page structure view with section labels.
7. Premium consulting deck with restrained color and strong type hierarchy.
8. Close-up of launch assets arranged like a tactical kit.

## Platform Specs

### Landing Page

- Desktop width: 1440px design baseline
- Mobile breakpoint: 390px baseline
- Hero height: Fit within first viewport while showing next section hint
- CTA button minimum height: 44px

### Social Launch Graphics

- LinkedIn post image: 1200 x 1200
- X/Twitter image: 1600 x 900
- Instagram square: 1080 x 1080
- Instagram story: 1080 x 1920

### PDF Or Delivery Deck

- PDF page: US Letter or A4
- Slide format: 16:9
- Minimum body size: 11pt in PDF, 18px equivalent in slides

## Do

- Make the product feel structured and premium.
- Show concrete launch artifacts.
- Use blue and mint as functional accents.
- Keep visual hierarchy sharp.
- Let the product system feel calm and decisive.

## Do Not

- Do not make it look like a generic AI SaaS landing page.
- Do not use vague abstract blobs, bokeh, or purple gradients.
- Do not overuse icons as decoration.
- Do not use fake scarcity badges.
- Do not make the page feel like a course marketplace template.
