# ux-audit

## Objective

The landing experience must make EMOVEL Launch Stack v1 feel like a premium, practical launch system rather than a loose collection of prompts.

## Primary UX Jobs

- Clarify the product within the first viewport.
- Show the transformation from scattered idea to launch-ready package.
- Make the deliverables concrete and inspectable.
- Explain why the $497 Core offer is cheaper and faster than hiring separate strategy, copy, and design help.
- Make checkout intent obvious without fake urgency.

## Recommended Page Flow

1. Hero with direct product promise and CTA.
2. Problem section: idea is unclear, not bad.
3. Seven-skill system strip.
4. Deliverables table.
5. Benefits tied to launch outcomes.
6. Value stack and pricing.
7. Guarantee.
8. FAQ.
9. Final CTA.

## Conversion Risks

- Product may be mistaken for a prompt pack unless outputs are shown clearly.
- Buyers may wonder whether they need a finished product. Address this in FAQ.
- The $497 price needs value context before checkout.
- Visual proof is still early; use concrete sample outputs and system screenshots.

## Required UX Decisions

- Keep one primary CTA label: Get The Launch Stack.
- Use tables for deliverables and pricing.
- Use compact process labels for the seven skills.
- Keep the guarantee close to pricing.
- Show Starter/Core/Premium only after Core value is clear.

## Mobile Requirements

- Hero headline must fit without clipping.
- CTA must appear above the fold.
- Tables should collapse into stacked rows.
- Pricing must remain readable on 390px screens.
